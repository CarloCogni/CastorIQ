How the Reliability tab works

What is it?

The is anything on fire? view — operator-side early-warning that surfaces a degraded provider, a writeback sandbox going sideways, or a stuck ingestion pipeline without you having to open Sentry, Django admin, or the Postgres console. Overview tells you what's happening; Cost tells you what it costs; Reliability tells you whether it's actually working.

KPI strip

Tile Reads
Success % Share of LLMCallLog calls with succeeded=True in the window. Yellow when below 95%.
Errors Failed LLM calls + ErrorLog rows in the window. Yellow when non-zero.
p95 latency · Ask 95th-percentile latency_ms for purpose='ask' calls in the window. Reads "—" when no calls have a measured latency yet.
p95 latency · Modify Same shape, for purpose='modify' calls.

The charts

Provider success One bar per provider. The dashed red line sits at 95% — anything below is a provider worth investigating. Providers with zero calls are hidden to keep the chart honest.
p95 latency Daily p95 latency for Ask (blue) and Modify (purple) over the last 30 days, regardless of the window selector. Gaps are intentional — days with no calls leave a break in the line rather than dropping to zero.
Error donut Top 10 categories from LLMCallLog.error_type (failed calls only) unioned with ErrorLog.exception_type. Same label on both sides collapses to one slice. Anything past the top 10 falls into "Other". Empty labels normalise to (unknown).

Unresolved error backlog

Counts every ErrorLog.is_resolved=False row — the count is absolute, not window-respecting. An open error matters whether it landed today or three weeks ago. The strip below splits the backlog into three age buckets:

<24h Recent — probably still in the operator's working memory.
24h–7d This week — should have an owner already.
>7d Stale — either a real bug or noise the resolution flow is dropping.

The table below the strip shows the most recent 10 unresolved rows; click the arrow icon to open the row in Django admin and either resolve it or annotate the resolution path.

Writeback failure taxonomy

Reads from metacastor.FailureRecord. The stacked bar shows failures per phase (VALIDATION / EXECUTION / SANDBOX), each segmented by category (RETRYABLE in amber, NON_RETRYABLE in red). The side table breaks the totals down by RSAA tier — VALIDATION failures that happen before a tier is chosen surface in the "— (early VALIDATION)" row. When nothing has failed in the window, the section quietly says so rather than drawing an empty chart.

Ingestion health

Documents and IFC files share the same lifecycle states (pending → processing → completed | failed). Each card shows total uploads, success % (completed ÷ terminal states — pending/processing don't count toward the denominator), and the failed count. The thin strip is a proportional bar of where the uploads currently sit; hover any segment for the count.

Caveats

error_type on LLMCallLog and exception_type on ErrorLog are free-form text — slices labeled identically collapse, slight typos do not. p95 needs at least 2 samples per (day, purpose) to be meaningful — a day with one call shows that call's latency, not a real percentile. nginx 4xx/5xx and Sentry deep-links are deliberately not on this tab. Add when a recurring symptom needs them, not preemptively. FailureRecord and writeback Conflict tables stay sparse until M5/M6 traffic — empty states are expected, not a bug.
