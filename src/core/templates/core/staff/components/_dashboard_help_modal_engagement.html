How the Engagement tab works

What is it?

The product-side view of Castor — how often users come back, how fast they reach first value, and whether they go beyond Ask into Modify. This is the page that turns "5 users, 6 weeks of beta" into a credible PMF story for a VC conversation. Charts populate as users sign up and return; sparse early, meaningful by week 4 of beta.

KPI strip

Tile Reads
DAU Distinct users who fired ≥1 LLM call today (UTC calendar day).
WAU / MAU Same idea, rolling last 7 / 30 days from now.
Stickiness DAU ÷ MAU. Healthy consumer products climb toward 50%+; tools that get used once a week sit closer to 15%. Pre-beta this is mostly noise.
Active (window) Distinct users with any LLM call in the selected window.
Power users Share of active users who created at least one ModificationProposal in the window. Proposal-generators are the deep-engagement segment — Ask-only users churn faster.

Cohort retention (the VC visual)

Each row is a sign-up week (ISO week of User.date_joined). Each column W0..W7 is the % of cohort members who fired ≥1 LLM call in that calendar week. Cells past "this week" stay blank rather than rendering as 0% — a cohort can't have a W3 number until W3 actually elapses. Read down a column to compare cohorts at the same age; read across a row to follow a cohort's curve. A flat 50%+ retention across 4 weeks is the cleanest single PMF signal an early-stage SaaS can produce.

DAU / WAU / MAU lines (60 days)

Three rolling distinct-user counts plotted daily. The window selector at the top doesn't change these — they always span 60 days so the lines have enough samples to read. MAU rises slowest and falls slowest; DAU jumps around; WAU sits in between. The shape that matters: MAU pulling away from DAU means new users are arriving but not coming back; the reverse means the base is consolidating.

Time-to-first-value + Feature mix

TTFV For every user, minutes from date_joined to their first successful Ask call (blue) and to their first proposal (purple), bucketed into <1h, 1h–1d, 1d–7d, 7d–30d, >30d, never. A wall of "<1h" means onboarding is clean; a wall of "never" means users are bouncing before activation.
Feature mix Last 30 days. Each active user lands in one of three rows — Ask only, Both, Modify only. The Both row is the deep-engagement segment; growing share of Both is the single best leading indicator of expansion revenue, post-monetisation.

Modify funnel by tier

Last 30 days of ModificationProposal rows, grouped by RSAA tier (T1 / T2 / T3, with an "untiered" column for rows that never picked a tier). Stages are pending → approved → applied → rejected → failed — each stage is its own coloured segment so you can read both within-tier progression and cross-tier patterns. The hypothesis under test (per the master plan): T1 lands ≥90% applied, T2 ≥70%, T3 ≥50%. Sparse during early beta — the section empty-states cleanly when no proposals exist.

Activity heatmap

LLM calls bucketed by hour-of-day × day-of-week, UTC. Useful to see if Castor is being used during workdays (which is what you'd expect for an AEC tool) or evenings-and-weekends (which would suggest hobbyist usage). Hot spots in Mon–Fri 09:00–17:00 UTC are the encouraging shape.

Caveats

DAU/WAU/MAU is computed over LLMCallLog, not last_login — a user who logged in but never fired a call doesn't count as active. Cohort cells past "this week" intentionally render blank, not 0%. Future weeks haven't elapsed. "First Ask" requires succeeded=True — a user whose only call failed lands in "never" until they retry. Stickiness needs MAU > 0 to be meaningful. Reads as "—" otherwise. Plotly is lazy-loaded only on this tab. Other tabs in the family stay on Chart.js to keep page weight low.
