How the Investor View works

What is it?

A screenshot-friendly composition of the five metrics that distinguish Castor from a generic SaaS dashboard. Audience is early-stage SaaS investors — VC partners scanning a deck slide. Every section has a numbered badge so the slide order stays intentional; the dateline at the top is the cropping line for the screenshot.

Header strip

Tile Reads
MAU Distinct users with ≥1 LLM call in the last 30 days. Pre-beta this is the design-partner count.
W4 retention (avg) Average W4 cell across all cohorts old enough to have one. Reads "—" until a cohort hits week 4.
Entities processed Sum of IFCFile.entity_count across successfully ingested files. Frames the engine scale.
Ollama-local share Local-vs-paid token mix over 30 days. Green when ≥80%.

The five sections

1 Cohort retention. Same heatmap as the Engagement tab — the single cleanest PMF signal for early SaaS. Read down a column to compare cohorts at the same age. Flat 50%+ retention across 4 weeks is the goal.
2 Proposal acceptance by tier. % of ModificationProposal rows that reached applied (collapsing approved + applied), broken out by RSAA tier. Hypothesis targets: T1 ≥90%, T2 ≥70%, T3 ≥50%. Bar turns green when target is met, amber otherwise — that's the VC-readable trust signal.
3 Token spend mix. Local (Ollama) vs paid (Anthropic, Groq, future cloud) over 30 days. ≥80% local is the "healthy gross margin" framing — paid token drift is the silent margin killer Castor's architecture is supposed to prevent.
4 IFC ingestion scatter. Each dot is one successfully parsed IFC file — x = entity count, y = parse latency in seconds. A flat-to-mildly-positive trend through 50k+ entities is the "engine scales" claim. Failed and in-flight files are excluded so the chart only shows real successful runs.
5 Design-partner engagement. Top 20 users by call volume last 30 days, with proposal count and last-seen timestamp. Likert (1–5) is intentionally not stored — you annotate it on the screenshot by hand. Investors only need the quantitative signal here; the qualitative goes in the talking points.

How to use it

Crop a screenshot starting at the dateline strip; everything below is composed to fit a single slide at standard 16:9. Empty states are quiet placeholders rather than empty charts — a section that can't yet show signal just notes that, rather than drawing a misleading "0 across the board" visual. The page composes existing helpers, so the numbers always match what the operational tabs (Overview, Cost, Engagement) show.

Caveats

This is staff-only and never linked from the public site. Sharing the URL with an investor would expose the rest of the dashboard family. W4 retention reads "—" until at least one cohort is old enough — don't screenshot the page before that, or be ready to explain "still pre-W4". Acceptance targets (T1 90 / T2 70 / T3 50) are the hypothesis under test, not commitments — flag them as such in the deck. Tier-`None` proposals (early-validation failures) are excluded from Section 2 by design — they're not a meaningful tier signal. IFC scatter only includes status='completed' files; in-flight or failed parses don't appear, by design.
