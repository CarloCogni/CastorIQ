How Work Orders work
Lifecycle

Each work order moves through ten stages: Draft → Submitted → Triaged → Assigned → Scheduled → In Progress → Completed → Verified → Closed. A parallel terminal stage, Cancelled, covers WOs that don't actually get done. Transitions write an immutable status event for the audit trail.

Three views
  • List — searchable / filterable card grid; bulk actions on multi-select.
  • Kanban — drag a card to move it to a new column; only valid transitions are accepted.
  • Calendar — month grid showing WOs by their scheduled_start.
Roles & permissions

Transitions are role-gated. The most common buckets:

  • Facilities Manager / Building Owner — triage, assign, verify, close, reopen, cancel.
  • Maintenance Engineer / Contractor — start & complete the WOs they're assigned to.
  • Anyone with project access — submit a draft WO; cancel their own submitted WO.

A maintenance engineer who reports their own WO can use the skip-triage path — a self-reported WO jumps directly from Draft to Assigned, no FM-side triage needed.

Anchors

A WO can be anchored to a FacilityAsset (linked to an IFC entity or orphan) and/or to a spatial container (room, floor, building). Both are optional but at least one is recommended for traceability.

Audit trail

Every status change writes a WorkOrderStatusEvent capturing the actor, their functional role at the time, and an optional note. These rows are append-only; the timeline on the WO detail is the source of truth.

Out of scope (M3)

SLA pause/resume, IFC export of closed WOs, condition-based PM triggers, and the BMS / sensor integration come in later milestones.
