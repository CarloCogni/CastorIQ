How Explore Works

What is Explore?

Explore is a 2D floor-plan workspace for facility managers. Drop points on a floor plan, attach photos or 360° panoramas to each one, and track how a space changes over time. Each point can link to an IFC IfcSpace so a click can drive the 3D viewer (coming soon) and a panel can pull rows from the Asset Register, Work Orders and Permits filtered to that room.

Floor plans

From IFC Auto-loaded. Each IfcBuildingStorey in the project's IFC model appears as a floor in the switcher. Rooms (IfcSpace children) are listed for each floor with their GlobalID and IFC properties — pick one in the right panel to link a point to it.
Import plans Upload images / PDFs. The toolbar's ⤓ Import plans button takes PNG / JPG / WebP / PDF; each PDF page becomes a floor. Plans uploaded for IFC storeys are persisted server-side and survive a browser refresh. The ⬚ White-out button strips a white background for clean line drawings (reversible).

Points & photos

+ Point Place pins. Click + Point then click anywhere on the plan to drop a pin. Drag to reposition. Each pin has its own phase (room state — Construction, Fit-out, Occupied by default; rename / recolour / add your own). Pin colour follows the phase; the number on the pin follows the placement order (or the in-phase sequence in "by phase" mode).
Photos & 360° Each point has two separate archives — flat Photos and equirectangular 360° panoramas. Upload from disk or capture from the device camera. The bottom timeline shows the archive's versions in time order; the panel hero is the newest. Click any timeline item to open the editor (name, code, date / time, description). Use ⇔ Compare to scrub two versions side-by-side with a draggable divider (works for both flat photos and synchronised 360° spheres).

IFC links & linked tables

In the right panel pick a room for the selected point to auto-fill its GlobalID and IFC type. You can also attach read-only linked tables to a room — open Work Orders, Facility Assets and active Permits — filtered by your chosen match key (GlobalID / room number / department). Clicking a placed pin currently fires a toast; cross-tool focus into the 3D viewer is on the roadmap.

Caveats

Explore stores 2D plan positions, not IFC geometry — placing a point never modifies the IFC file. Floor plans uploaded for non-IFC floors (e.g. PDF pages you imported manually) live in your browser only; only IFC-anchored floors persist server-side in V1. 360° panoramas should be equirectangular (≈2:1). The viewer warns if an image isn't. PDF import and the 360° viewer load pdf.js and three.js from a CDN — the iframe needs internet for those. Photo storage is unbounded in V1 — large rollouts may want to revisit quotas.
