How Permits Work

What is a permit?

A permit is a regulatory or safety instrument required before certain work can be performed — hot work, confined-space entry, electrical isolation, working at height, or a project-specific other category. Castor tracks the instrument itself (number, issuing party, validity window) and which work orders it authorizes.

Lifecycle

Draft Captured but not yet in force — use while negotiating dates with the authority.
Active In effect. Within its validity window the linked work can proceed.
Expired Past valid_until. Use the Expiring soon badge (shown 0–90 days before expiry) to catch renewals early.
Revoked Withdrawn by the authority or the project. Use Revoke on the detail page; a dated note is appended to the permit's notes.

Linking to work orders

Open a work order and use the Linked permits section to attach an existing permit. One permit can authorize several WOs (a single hot-work permit may cover three roof-deck WOs for the same week). Unlink from the WO side; the permit itself is unaffected.

Who can do what

Creating, editing, revoking, and linking requires Editor or Owner access. Viewers can see the permit list and a WO's linked permits but cannot mutate either side.

Caveats

Permit numbers are unique per project. Re-using the authority's number across projects is fine. The 90-day expiring-soon window is a client-side signal only — the permit does not auto-revoke at expiry. Permits are DB-only records — they are not exported to IFC in the current milestone.
