Context Window & LLM Performance

LLMs do not process context uniformly. Performance degrades as the context window fills up — even on simple retrieval tasks. This is known as Context Rot.

Chroma Research Paper 14 Jul 2025 Watch Video 11 Mar 2026

The graph above (from Chroma's research) shows how leading models — including Claude, GPT-4.1, Qwen3, and Gemini 2.5 — lose reliability as token count grows. Real-world tasks with ambiguous queries degrade faster than synthetic benchmarks suggest.

Practical advice: responses may degrade above 50% usage. Start a new session when nearing the limit to maintain quality.

How the % is estimated: token count ≈ characters ÷ 4, divided by the model's usable budget (90% of context window − 1,500 tokens reserved for output). This is conservative — IFC data (GUIDs, long property chains) is token-denser than prose, so actual consumption often exceeds the estimate.

The bar reflects the full context: system prompt + RAG-retrieved chunks + conversation history — not just your last message.
