# Castor Screenplay — *"Castor vs. The Jargon Beast"*

> A 2-minute-30-second animated mascot short for Castor's marketing surface.
> Production target: Motionfly.co or comparable AI animation service.
> Standalone marketing piece — not the official beta-launch reel.

---

## Logline

When a swirling tornado of BIM jargon erupts out of a freshly delivered IFC file and terrorizes an architecture office, a quiet beaver in a hard hat shows up, chews the jargon down to plain English, and builds a dam that lets humans finally talk to their buildings — both ways.

---

## Tone & References

- **Primary reference:** the AI-generated Super Mario shorts circulating on social — bright, exaggerated, breaking the fourth wall, sight-gag forward.
- **Secondary reference:** *Charlie Chaplin*. Our hero **does not speak.** Castor expresses everything through posture, eyebrows, hat-tipping, chomps, and burps. The comedy is physical.
- **Tertiary reference:** Pixar shorts (*Piper*, *Bao*) for emotional beats around the silent protagonist.
- **The vibe:** *fun first, accurate second, jargon-free always.* This is marketing, not an engineering briefing.

## Cast & Design Notes

### Castor (hero, silent)

- Brown beaver, plush proportions (think Pixar-friendly, not Geico-realistic).
- **Tiny yellow construction hard hat** with a Castor-blue stripe.
- **Castor-blue scarf** (`#3b82f6`) — the only intense blue in the frame so he always reads as the focal point.
- Big bucked teeth, visible when chomping.
- Tiny clipboard tucked under one arm in early shots.
- A tool-belt with: a chisel, a measuring tape, a tiny Python scroll, a quill.
- **Animation note:** ONE master character reference image must be generated up-front and re-used for every shot prompt. AI animation tools drift on character consistency; lock him visually before any motion work.

### The Jargon Beast (antagonist)

- **Not a creature with a body** — a swirling, chaotic *tornado* of floating glowing acronyms and IFC syntax fragments.
- Symbols inside the swirl: `IFCSPACE`, `Pset_WallCommon`, `QTO_QuantityArea`, `IfcRelAggregates`, `IfcWall.PredefinedType`, `BIM`, `LOD`, `GUID`, `IFC4x3`.
- Color: angry neon orange / red — the *opposite* of Castor-blue.
- **Why a swirl, not a creature:** AI animation tools fail at character consistency. A morphing chaos cloud is *supposed* to look different in every frame. The genre rescues the tooling limitation.

### The IFC Cube (NOT the villain)

- A serene, semi-transparent **glowing cube**, soft white-blue interior, faint wireframe edges.
- Floats. Hums softly.
- **Never harmed in the film.** When Castor "processes" things, the cube *glows brighter* — never smaller, never broken. This visually encodes the "IFC file is source of truth" design principle.

### Supporting cast

- **Architect** — woman, ~30, glasses, holding a tablet. Drops the tablet in shock.
- **Engineer** — man, ~40, hi-vis vest, hard hat, clipboard. Comically overprepared but useless against the Beast.
- **Client** — older man, suit, briefcase. The one who eventually asks Castor to *change* something. Gets one spoken line.
- **Intern** — young, oversized hoodie, holding three coffees. Faints in Act 1, wakes in Act 2 to cheer.
- **Courier** — appears only in opening shot. Hands over the crate. Exits.

### Color palette

- **Castor blue** `#3b82f6` — heroes, answers, "good"
- **Jargon orange-red** — beast, confusion, "bad"
- **Soft white-blue** — the IFC cube, neutral
- **Beige / paper tones** — the office, blueprints
- **Black with white text** — intertitles (silent-film style)

## The Screenplay

> Format notes: **INT.** = interior, **EXT.** = exterior. **INTERTITLE** = silent-film text card on solid black background, white serif font, ~1.5 seconds on screen. **SFX** = sound effect. **MUSIC** cue is one continuous chiptune track unless otherwise noted.

---

### ACT ONE — THE ERUPTION (0:00 – 0:45)

---

**FADE IN.**

**0:00–0:04 — SHOT 1 — INT. ARCHITECTURE OFFICE — DAY (wide)**

A bright, slightly stylized open-plan office. Drafting tables. A 3D model on a screen. Coffee cups. The **ARCHITECT**, **ENGINEER**, and **CLIENT** stand around a table. The **INTERN** approaches with a tray of three coffees.

MUSIC: cheerful chiptune begins, mid-tempo.

INTERTITLE (1.0s):
> **A perfectly normal morning at the architecture office.**

---

**0:04–0:09 — SHOT 2 — A KNOCK AT THE DOOR**

A **COURIER** wheels in a wooden crate stamped with bold letters:

> **IFC FILE — HANDLE WITH CARE**

The team gathers around it, eyes wide with excitement.

SFX: doorbell chime, hand-cart wheels squeaking.

---

**0:09–0:14 — SHOT 3 — THE CRATE OPENS**

The crate hinges open with a soft *whoosh*. A serene, glowing **IFC CUBE** floats out gently, hovering at eye level. Pale blue-white glow. Soft hum.

The Architect smiles. The Engineer leans in, curious. The Intern almost drops the coffees.

SFX: gentle harp glissando + low hum.

INTERTITLE (1.2s):
> **The building, delivered as data.**

---

**0:14–0:20 — SHOT 4 — THE ERUPTION**

Without warning, the cube **PULSES** once — and from its surface erupts the **JARGON BEAST**: a screaming tornado of glowing orange-red acronyms whipping into the air.

`IFCSPACE` `Pset_WallCommon` `QTO_QuantityArea` `IfcRelAggregates` `BIM` `IFCWALL.PredefinedType=PARTITIONING`

The symbols swirl violently around the room.

MUSIC: chiptune *records-scratch* drop, then transitions to a frantic minor-key chase variant.
SFX: tornado roar, paper rustle, glass shatter (offscreen).

---

**0:20–0:30 — SHOT 5 — CHAOS MONTAGE (rapid cuts)**

- **Architect** drops her tablet. It shatters on the floor.
- **Engineer** scrambles for a thick BIM manual — opens it — every page is blank. He flips it upside down. Still blank.
- **Client** tries to read a floating `Pset_FireRating` shard. His glasses **crack down the middle** with a comedic *plink!*
- **Intern** stares at the tornado, eyes wide, then slowly **faints backwards** out of frame, coffees still upright in her hand as she goes down.

SFX: glass crack, comedic *thunk*, three coffee splashes.

---

**0:30–0:36 — SHOT 6 — THE TEAM CORNERED**

The three conscious adults huddle behind an overturned drafting table. Above them, the jargon storm fills the entire upper half of the frame. Speech bubbles full of `???` pop above their heads.

INTERTITLE (1.5s):
> **The model held the answers.**
> **But nobody could read it.**

---

**0:36–0:45 — SHOT 7 — THE FROZEN SHARD**

A single jargon fragment drifts forward, slowing — slower — until it freezes mid-air directly in front of the camera:

> `IfcWall.PredefinedType = PARTITIONING`

The team stares at it. The Architect mouths "what does that even *mean?*"

Beat of silence. The chiptune drops to a single sustained note.

**HARD CUT TO BLACK.**

---

### ACT TWO — ENTER THE BEAVER (0:45 – 1:30)

---

**0:45–0:50 — SHOT 8 — THE DOOR**

Black. Then: a soft creak. A door in the corner of the frame swings open. Sunlight pours through. Silhouette: a small, round figure with a hard hat.

SFX: door creak. A single high chiptune chime — *the hero is here*.

MUSIC: returns, this time a confident heroic chiptune variant with a steady bassline.

INTERTITLE (1.2s):
> **Enter Castor.**

---

**0:50–0:56 — SHOT 9 — CASTOR'S WALK-IN**

**CASTOR** strolls in. Calm, unhurried. Hard hat low over his eyes. Blue scarf trailing. Clipboard tucked under one arm. The tornado still rages above the team, but Castor doesn't even look up at it.

He stops. Tips his hat to the camera. A single confident eyebrow raise.

The team turns. They see him. The Architect's jaw drops. The Engineer salutes for no reason.

SFX: tiny footstep patters, single hat-tip whoosh.

---

**0:56–1:02 — SHOT 10 — THE FIRST CHOMP**

Castor reaches up and plucks a single `IFCSPACE` shard out of the swirling storm — handling it like an apple. He examines it. Sniffs it. Shrugs.

**CHOMP.**

He swallows. The chiptune pauses. He blinks. The team stares.

Then — a tidy little *burp* — and a **clean white speech bubble** pops out above his head with crisp serif text:

> **"Lobby — 450 m²."**

Beat. The Architect's eyes go wide.

SFX: cartoon chomp, satisfied burp, magic *ding!* on the speech bubble appearance.

---

**1:02–1:14 — SHOT 11 — THE CHOMP MONTAGE**

Sped-up. Chiptune surges into its main melodic theme.

Castor goes to work. Each cut: a chomp, a burp, an answer bubble. Mario-style coin "ding!" on every successful translation.

- CHOMP → **"Fire rating — 2 hours."**
- CHOMP → **"Material — concrete C30."**
- CHOMP → **"45 doors. 12 fire-rated."**
- CHOMP → **"Total floor area — 8,400 m²."**
- CHOMP → **"Window-to-wall ratio — 38%."**

Each answer bubble drifts gently down to the team, who catch them like falling leaves. The Engineer holds one up to the light. The Architect hugs one to her chest. The Intern wakes up, sits up, sees Castor, and **applauds**.

SFX: coin ding × 5, distant cheers.

---

**1:14–1:22 — SHOT 12 — THE DAM**

Castor doesn't waste the chewed-up jargon. He gathers the splintered acronym-wood, drags it to the corner of the room, and — fast-motion construction beaver style — **builds a small dam**.

But this dam isn't holding water back. It has **two channels carved through it**:

- The **left channel** flows from the team → into the IFC cube. Labelled in the wood: **QUESTIONS →**
- The **right channel** flows from the cube → back to the team. Labelled: **← ANSWERS**

The cube glows brighter. The team gathers around the dam, awestruck.

MUSIC: chiptune theme reaches a triumphant chorus.

INTERTITLE (1.5s):
> **Your building, in plain English.**

---

**1:22–1:30 — SHOT 13 — A PERFECT MOMENT**

Wide shot. Team smiling. Castor casually leans on his dam, examining his clipboard. Speech bubbles full of plain-English answers float gently above everyone. The IFC cube hums contently.

The Jargon Beast tornado is **gone**.

A beat of calm. The team starts to relax — until —

---

### ACT THREE — THE TWIST: HE BUILDS TOO (1:30 – 2:10)

---

**1:30–1:38 — SHOT 14 — THE CLIENT STEPS FORWARD**

The **CLIENT** clears his throat. He shuffles toward Castor, briefcase in hand, eyes hopeful.

This is the one spoken line of the entire film. Delivered with quiet, almost-childlike sincerity:

CLIENT
*"Castor… can you also* ***change*** *something?"*

The chiptune pauses. Everyone leans in. Castor's eyebrow raises *slowly*.

---

**1:38–1:46 — SHOT 15 — THE CHISEL**

Castor's hand drops to his tool-belt. He produces a tiny silver **chisel**. He hefts it. He winks at the camera.

He turns to the IFC cube, taps one of its glowing faces with the chisel.

SFX: tiny *tink!* — the sound of intent.

A holographic **PROPERTY CARD** materializes in the air beside the cube:

> **Fire Rating**
> ~~1 hour~~ → **2 hours**
> [ ✓ Approve ] [ ✗ Reject ]

---

**1:46–1:52 — SHOT 16 — APPROVAL**

The Client looks at Castor. Castor gestures to the card: *"your call, friend."*

The Client reaches out and taps **✓ Approve.**

The cube glows **brighter still**. A tiny **GIT COMMIT STAMP** falls from above and lands on a ledger book that materializes briefly beside the dam:

> `commit a7f2c91 — fire-rating updated`

The stamp emits a satisfying *clunk*.

SFX: approval chime, commit stamp *clunk*, faint applause.

---

**1:52–2:02 — SHOT 17 — THE BIGGER CHANGE**

The Client, emboldened, gestures at the cube again — a bigger ask.

Castor nods seriously. This time he doesn't reach for the chisel. He reaches deeper into his tool-belt and pulls out a tiny rolled **PYTHON SCROLL** with code symbols glowing on its surface.

He unrolls it and **holds it up for the Client to see first**. A bright banner appears across the top of the scroll:

> **HUMAN REVIEW REQUIRED**

The Client adjusts his (still-cracked) glasses, reads it carefully, nods, and taps **✓ Approve.**

Magic sparkles. The cube glows brighter again. A second commit stamp falls.

INTERTITLE (1.8s):
> **Castor never decides on his own.**
> **You always sign off.**

---

**2:02–2:10 — SHOT 18 — THE EXPANDED DAM**

Camera pulls back. The dam has grown. Now it has **three labelled compartments** stamped into the wood:

> **ASK** | **CHANGE** | **AUDIT**

Each compartment glows softly. The team is gathered around it like it's a campfire. The Intern raises a (fresh) coffee to Castor in a toast. Castor tips his hat in return.

MUSIC: chiptune theme reaches its final, satisfying resolution.

---

### OUTRO — THE LOCKUP (2:10 – 2:30)

---

**2:10–2:18 — SHOT 19 — WIDE PULL-BACK**

Camera pulls back through the office window. Outside, we see the office building itself — and floating gently above its roof, three plain-English answer-bubbles drifting up into a blue sky:

> *"All systems documented."*
> *"Every change logged."*
> *"Ask anything."*

---

**2:18–2:28 — SHOT 20 — LOGO LOCKUP**

Cut to clean Castor-blue background. The CASTOR wordmark assembles letter by letter (chiptune *power-up* sound). Tiny silhouette of Castor sits at the corner of the wordmark, looking up at his own name.

Centered text:

> **CASTOR**
> *Your building, in plain English.*
>
> **castoriq.io** — beta access by invitation.

---

**2:28–2:30 — SHOT 21 — THE BUTTON**

Castor turns to the camera. Tips his hat one final time. Gives a tiny **thumbs-up.**

SFX: classic Mario-style coin *ding!*

**FADE TO BLACK.**

## Production Notes for Motionfly (or equivalent)

### Pre-production checklist

1. **Lock the Castor character sheet first.** Generate a single master reference image of Castor (front, three-quarter, side, back) BEFORE generating any shots. Include this image as a reference in every subsequent shot prompt. AI animation tools drift; the reference image is your insurance.
2. **Generate the IFC Cube once.** Same reasoning. One reference asset, reused everywhere.
3. **Do NOT lock the Jargon Beast.** Let it morph. Inconsistent jargon-tornado design is a *feature* — the chaos reads as intentional. Different acronyms swirl in each shot.
4. **Storyboard the 21 shots above first.** Generate stills. Approve stills. Then animate. Cheaper than re-animating after the fact.

### Shot-prompt template

For each shot, build the AI prompt as:

```
[CASTOR REFERENCE IMAGE]
[IFC CUBE REFERENCE IMAGE — if in shot]
[Setting: office interior, drafting tables, soft beige tones, Castor-blue accent]
[Action: <shot description from screenplay>]
[Camera: <wide / medium / close-up>]
[Mood: <triumphant / chaotic / calm>]
[Style: Pixar-meets-8-bit, exaggerated cartoon proportions]
[Avoid: photorealism, realistic beaver fur detail, dark/gritty palette]
```

### Audio production

- **One chiptune track**, ~2:30 long, with three sections: calm intro → chaotic minor-key middle → triumphant resolution. Source from a royalty-free library (Pixabay Music, Free Music Archive, Incompetech — Kevin MacLeod's "Hero Down" or similar). **Cost: zero.**
- **SFX library:** classic cartoon pack + Mario-style ding/coin sounds. Royalty-free.
- **VO recording for the Client's single line:** 5 seconds of studio time. Hire a single voice actor for one take. **Cost: ~€30–€80 on Fiverr.**
- **Intertitle font:** a classic serif like "Linotype Didot" or free alternative "Playfair Display." Black background, white text, drop-in templates available in any video editor.

## Verification (does the cut work?)

1. **The two-cousin test.** Show to a 12-year-old and a 60-year-old who don't know what IFC is. After one viewing, can both answer: *"What does Castor do?"* — and *"Did you smile or laugh at least once?"* Yes to both = working.
2. **The mute test.** Mute the audio. Does Act 3 (Modify + human review + commit stamp) still read visually? If not, the intertitles need to do more work, or the holographic property card needs to be larger.
3. **The replay test.** Would a stranger voluntarily watch it a second time? If no, the chomp montage and dam-build aren't landing — they're the visual joy of the piece.
4. **The drift test.** Watch with character-consistency in mind. Does Castor look like the same beaver in every shot? If not, regenerate the offending shots with stricter reference adherence.
