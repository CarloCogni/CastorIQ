What a facility manager actually needs

Layer 1 — Daily Without these, nobody opens the system
Fault reporting
Tenant photographs the issue, submits it, manager sees it immediately
Technician assignment
Who is available, where they are, what they already have on their plate
Live work status
Open / in progress / waiting for part / completed
Push notifications
New fault, approaching deadline, escalation after X hours without action
Quick contacts
Emergency numbers, contractor reachable in one tap
Keys & access
Who holds which key, chip access records, key issuance log
Layer 2 — Weekly Skip these and things start falling apart
Inspection calendar
Lifts, boilers, fire extinguishers — alert 30 days before expiry
Handover records
Service sheet, photo evidence, mobile signature on completion
Spare parts inventory
What's in stock, what to reorder, from which supplier
Cost per work order
Labour + materials + transport, invoice attached
Contracts & deadlines
Leases, service agreements, expiry dates visible at a glance
Photo documentation
Before/after condition, geotagged, timestamped, with author
Layer 3 — Monthly Management & compliance
Owner report
What was done, what it cost, what is coming up next
H&S and fire safety docs
All valid certificates in one place, scanned originals attached
Energy & utilities
Electricity, water, gas — year-on-year consumption trend
Daily — critical path
Weekly — operational
Monthly — management
